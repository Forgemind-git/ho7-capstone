# HO7 — Mid-Course Capstone — End-to-End AI Solution

> Hands-on portfolio project · **Week 3** · **Peer-reviewed** · module M8. Part of the **ForgeMind AI — AI Productivity Essentials** course.

## Goal

**Done when:** Combines ≥3 concepts · solves a real problem · reproducible

## What to ship

The solution repo + a story-driven README (problem→approach→tools→result) + a 3–5 min Loom + a 'concepts used' checklist.

## Pick a problem statement

Choose **one** of these real use-cases — or bring your own (get it approved first):

1. Your support team is buried in tickets and leadership has no view of what is breaking. Build an AI support-triage system combining an LLM eval (which model classifies best), a tagging prompt library, and a no-code automation, ending in a simple trends view. End result: tickets get auto-categorised and you can show a one-screen summary of what is going wrong.

2. As a marketer you cannot keep up with what rivals are shipping. Build a competitor-intelligence tracker combining NotebookLM research on their material, a grounded assistant that answers questions with citations, and a Skill-driven automation that compiles a gap analysis. End result: a cited brief on where you are behind, refreshed on demand from three combined concepts.

3. Hiring is slow because every recruiter screens differently. Build a hiring co-pilot combining a grounded assistant on the role and company, a screening prompt library with a consistent rubric, and an automation that compiles candidate notes. End result: each candidate is screened the same way and you can show a pipeline summary, combining grounding, prompting and automation.

4. Your content output stalls because research, drafting and repurposing are all manual. Build a content engine combining trend research, a prompt library for drafts, and a repurposing automation, ending in a simple performance view. End result: a topic goes from research to ready-to-post variants with minimal hand-holding, combining four course concepts.

5. You never know where your money goes until the month ends. Build a personal-finance copilot combining an ingest step for your statements, a categorisation automation, and a grounded Q&A assistant over the data. End result: you can ask 'how much on food last month?' and get a sourced answer, combining automation, grounding and prompting.
