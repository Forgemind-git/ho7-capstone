AUDIENCE PERSONAS — FlowSpace Content

=======================================================
PERSONA 1: "THE OVERLOADED OPS MANAGER" (Primary)
=======================================================

Name: Alex (gender-neutral)
Title: Operations Manager / Head of Operations
Company size: 50–200 employees
Industry: SaaS, professional services, agencies

BACKGROUND
Alex manages a team of 8-15 people. They're responsible for keeping projects on track, processes running smoothly, and people aligned. They didn't plan to be in ops — they grew into it from a project management or coordinator role.

GOALS
- Get the team to move faster without burning people out
- Reduce the amount of time they spend in meetings and on Slack
- Have visibility into what everyone's working on without micromanaging
- Make better decisions faster using real data

FRUSTRATIONS
- "I spend 60% of my time in meetings that could have been an email"
- "I don't have time to think — I'm always reacting"
- "My team uses 12 different tools and nothing talks to each other"
- "By the time I figure out what everyone's working on, something's already on fire"

CONTENT CONSUMPTION
- Reads newsletters: TLDR, Morning Brew, Lenny's Newsletter
- Follows: Adam Grant, Annie Duke, Claire Hughes Johnson on LinkedIn
- Consumes content during commute (podcast-friendly) or early morning
- Shares content with their team when it's immediately actionable
- Skims first, reads fully if the first section is compelling

WHAT THEY WANT FROM CONTENT
- Specific tactics they can implement this week, not in "6 months"
- Real examples from real companies (case studies beat generic advice)
- Permission to do less, not a list of more things to do
- Templates, frameworks, and scripts they can use immediately

=======================================================
PERSONA 2: "THE SCALING TEAM LEAD" (Secondary)
=======================================================

Name: Jordan
Title: Team Lead, Tech Lead, Engineering Manager
Company size: 20–100 employees
Industry: SaaS, startup

BACKGROUND
Jordan recently moved from individual contributor to people manager. They're still writing code (or doing their IC work) 40-60% of the time. Managing people is new and sometimes uncomfortable.

GOALS
- Help their team deliver consistently without constant oversight
- Build a culture of ownership and accountability
- Grow their own management skills while staying technically credible
- Make space for deep work in their own schedule

FRUSTRATIONS
- "I feel guilty when I'm heads-down because I worry I'm not accessible enough"
- "My team comes to me for every decision — I want them to be more independent"
- "I don't know how to give hard feedback without damaging the relationship"
- "I got promoted because I was good at the job, not because I knew how to manage"

CONTENT CONSUMPTION
- Reads: Manager Tools, Lara Hogan's blog, HBR (selectively)
- Active on LinkedIn; lurks on Twitter/X
- Prefers shorter content — newsletters over long blog posts
- Buys books but rarely finishes them

WHAT THEY WANT FROM CONTENT
- Reassurance that they're not alone in finding management hard
- Specific scripts for difficult conversations
- Simple frameworks they can remember without notes
- Success stories from managers at similar companies
